## Brand Positioning

Tømrer & Snedker v/ Martin Mejdahl Jørgensen ApS positions as the reliable, warranty-backed carpentry specialist for Danish homeowners who want skilled tradework done properly — whether it's a new build in Jutland or a renovation in Copenhagen.

**Company DNA:** "100% dansk håndværk" (100% Danish craftsmanship)

**Competitive advantages:**

- **Byg Garanti warranty** — Consumer-grade warranty protection (up to 10 years on hidden defects) gives private clients security that most smaller tradespeople can't offer. This is a real differentiator.
- **Full-scope capability** — Combines structural carpentry (tømrer) and finish joinery (snedker) in one firm. Clients don't need two contractors.
- **Nationwide reach from Holstebro** — Demonstrated by the Copenhagen project; not a local-only firm despite the Jutland address.
- **Transparent process** — Site emphasizes that Martin consults before starting to ensure clients get the right solution, not just the most expensive one.
- **Competitive pricing with professional standards** — Explicitly positioned as competing on price without cutting corners on craft quality.

**Messaging rules:**
- Never lead with generic quality claims ("high quality solutions") — they are assumed baseline, not differentiators
- Always lead with concrete proof: warranty coverage, specific project types, the Dansk Byggeri membership
- Attract customers who want a trustworthy tradesperson, not the cheapest quote or a faceless company
- Use the CVR number and Byg Garanti as trust anchors — Danish homeowners know what these mean

## Brand Attributes

The website should communicate these qualities:

- **Trustworthiness** — Byg Garanti certification, CVR transparency, and willingness to consult before committing all signal a contractor who doesn't cut corners or disappear after payment
- **Genuine craftsmanship** — "100% dansk håndværk" is the core claim; the site should feel like a skilled tradesperson's work, not a corporate construction firm
- **Versatility** — The case studies show new residential build, structural extension, and specialist mold remediation — this firm handles the full breadth of carpentry challenges
- **Accessibility** — A single phone number and email, a named person (Martin), small-firm feel; clients can reach the actual craftsperson, not a call centre
- **Reliability** — Nationwide operations, long-standing Dansk Byggeri membership, and the Byg Garanti warranty all reinforce that this business delivers and stands behind its work

## Design Constraints

- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- **Performance:** Server Components by default, optimized images, fast LCP
- **Multilingual:** Danish (primary language). Layouts must accommodate Danish text length. No English version exists yet — add as future phase if client requests.
- **Mobile-responsive:** Design for both desktop and mobile/tablet visitors — tradespeople and their clients often browse on mobile
- **Authentic feel:** This is a one-person-named business (Martin Mejdahl Jørgensen is in the company name). The site must feel like a real craftsperson's business, not a generic construction firm. Real project photos over stock imagery wherever possible.

## Tone of Voice — Principles

- **Direct and concrete** — Danish trade culture values plain speech. Say what the job is, what it costs (or how to find out), and what the client gets. Avoid marketing fluff.
- **First-person, named** — The company name includes a person's name. Copy can use "vi" (we) or reference Martin directly. It's not a faceless firm.
- **Technical confidence without jargon** — Homeowners don't know trade terminology. Name the service clearly; don't oversell the complexity.
- **Warm but not casual** — Danish professional register: friendly and approachable, but not slangy. Use "du" (you) freely, as is standard in Danish B2C.
- **Proof over promise** — Back every claim with evidence: Byg Garanti for quality, Dansk Byggeri for credibility, project photos for capability. Never assert without demonstrating.

**Per language:**
- **Danish:** Professional but warm, "vi"-centric, use "du" in direct address, lead with Byg Garanti and Dansk Byggeri as shorthand trust signals that Danish readers immediately recognize
- **English (if added later):** Slightly more formal, explain Byg Garanti and Dansk Byggeri since non-Danish readers won't know the schemes; translate service names with brief explanations
