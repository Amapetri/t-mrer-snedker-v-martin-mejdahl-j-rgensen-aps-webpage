3. **Works nationwide, based in Holstebro** — From Jutland to Copenhagen (proven by the CPH apartment case). A homeowner outside Midtjylland is not out of reach.

4. **Member of Dansk Byggeri** — The professional trade association membership signals that this firm operates to documented industry standards, carries appropriate insurance, and is accountable to an industry body.

- CVR: 3646 6588 — publicly verifiable, transparent, not a fly-by-night operation
- Real project photography from actual completed jobs (not stock imagery)

## 2. Audience Segments

### Segment A: The Renovation Planner (Primary)

**Who they are:** Danish homeowner, 35–60, owner-occupied house in Jutland (primarily Holstebro, Herning, Struer, Ringkøbing area). Planning a significant project: roof renovation, house extension, window replacement, or full interior renovation. Has budget awareness, has probably had one bad contractor experience or heard stories from a neighbour.

**What they're trying to accomplish:** Get a reliable, skilled craftsperson who will show up, do the job right, and not disappear when problems emerge. They want the project done once, not redone.

**Primary anxiety / objection:** "How do I know this contractor is legitimate and will stand behind the work?" Price is secondary to trust. They fear sunk cost — paying a contractor who delivers poor work and is then unreachable.

**What converts them:** The Byg Garanti certification explained plainly: *"3 år synlige fejl, 10 år skjulte fejl — vi stiller garanti for det vi laver."* Combine with the CVR number and Dansk Byggeri membership as corroborating trust signals. Show real project photos, not stock imagery.

**Pages/sections that speak to them:** Homepage hero, About (who is Martin), Services (what they need), any Byg Garanti explanation panel.

---

### Segment B: The Problem Owner (High Urgency)

**Who they are:** Homeowner who has discovered a specific defect — visible mold (skimmelsvamp), roof leak, storm damage, rotted window frames. Could be anywhere in Denmark (the CPH apartment case proves this). Urgency is higher; they are searching with a problem in mind, not a project.

**What they're trying to accomplish:** Diagnosis, remediation, confidence it won't recur. They want an expert, not just a labourer.

**Primary anxiety / objection:** "Is this contractor qualified to handle this specific problem, or are they just a general builder who will make things worse?" Mold remediation in particular requires correct technique or the problem returns.

**What converts them:** Evidence of having done this before (the Copenhagen mold case), combined with the warranty covering latent defects. The explicit service category "fugtskade sanering" must be findable by search. A before/after narrative on the case study page is the strongest possible proof.

**Pages/sections that speak to them:** Services (fugtskade sanering specifically), Cases (CPH apartment before/after), Contact (fast call to action).

---

### Segment C: The New Build / Extension Client (Higher Value)

**Who they are:** Homeowner planning a significant structural project — a garage, a house extension (tilbygning), a complete new-build of a small residential structure. Often has an architect drawing or a municipality permit already. More sophisticated buyer; they know the difference between a general builder and a tømrer.

**What they're trying to accomplish:** A single contractor who handles the structural shell (tømrer) and all carpentry finish (snedker) without hand-off complexity. Professional project management, not ad-hoc scheduling.

**Primary anxiety / objection:** "Will this firm handle the whole scope, or do I end up coordinating five different trades myself?" Scope gaps and timeline slippage are their primary fears.

**What converts them:** The explicit positioning of tømrer + snedker as a combined capability. Case studies showing new-build and extension work (parcelhus Holstebro, tilbygning Halgård). The Dansk Byggeri membership signals professional project execution.

**Pages/sections that speak to them:** Services (tilbygninger, garager, nybyggeri), Cases (parcelhus and tilbygning cases), About (Martin's background as a craftsman with both competencies).

**What:** Residential carpentry and joinery — from structural shell to fine finish, including specialist remediation — delivered by a named, warranty-backed craftsman.

**Who:** Danish homeowners planning renovations, extensions, or new builds who need a contractor they can trust and verify.

- Real project photography: three documented case studies with before/during/after imagery

**What differentiates from generic contractors:**

- Byg Garanti coverage on hidden defects (10 years) is rare for a small firm and directly answers the homeowner's deepest fear: discovering a defect years after completion
- Personal accessibility — clients communicate with Martin, not with an office
- Nationwide reach despite small-firm character: the Copenhagen case study proves this is not aspirational

## 5. Tone of Voice Guide

### Register

**Semi-formal, personal, direct.** Martin is named in the company name. The site speaks as "vi" (we) in most body copy, but can reference Martin by name in the About section and anywhere personal accountability is the point. Use "du" freely when addressing the visitor — this is standard and expected in Danish B2C.

Not: a corporate construction conglomerate.
Not: a lad with a van and no business registration.
Exactly: a skilled sole trader running a proper business who speaks to you like a professional, not a salesperson.

### Technical Depth

Accessible, not dumbed down. Use trade terms where they are the natural Danish words (tag-renovering, tilbygning, skimmelsvamp, fugtskade) — these are not jargon, they are the words homeowners use when searching Google. Avoid internal trade acronyms or construction specification language that homeowners would not recognise.

### Emotional Register

**Safety and reliability.** The homeowner's primary emotion is apprehension — renovations are expensive, the stakes are high, bad contractors exist. The site's emotional job is to defuse that anxiety through credibility signals and evidence. The feeling after reading should be: *"This is clearly a legitimate, accountable craftsperson. I can trust this."*

Secondary emotion: **capability confidence.** The cases and service breadth should leave the visitor feeling that whatever their project, Martin has done something comparable before.

### What to Avoid

- "Høj kvalitet" as a standalone claim — it means nothing without proof
- "Vi stræber efter at..." — vague aspiration language
- "Vi er et dynamisk firma med..." — corporate boilerplate
- Superlatives without evidence: "den bedste," "den mest erfarne"
- Passive voice and impersonal constructions that remove Martin from the picture
- Any English-language business phrases translated literally into Danish

### On-Brand Phrases (direction, not final copy)

1. *"Du ved hvem der bygger dit hus — og du er dækket, hvis noget ikke er som det skal."*
   (You know who is building your home — and you are covered if something isn't right.)

2. *"Tømrer og snedker under samme tag: ét firma, ét ansvar."*
   (Carpenter and joiner under one roof: one firm, one responsibility.)

3. *"Gratis tilbud, ingen forpligtelse — lad os kigge på opgaven sammen."*
   (Free quote, no obligation — let us look at the job together.)

4. *"Fra Holstebro til København — vi arbejder hele landet."*
   (From Holstebro to Copenhagen — we work all of Denmark.)

5. *"Byg Garanti: 3 år på synlige fejl, 10 år på skjulte fejl. Det er ikke et løfte — det er en garanti."*
   (Byg Garanti: 3 years on visible defects, 10 years on hidden defects. That is not a promise — it is a guarantee.)

### Off-Brand Phrases (what to eliminate)

1. *"Vi leverer høj kvalitet til konkurrencedygtige priser"* — the canonical bad trade copy, says nothing
2. *"Vi er din partner i byggebranchen"* — corporate partnership language, not human
3. *"Med mange års erfaring i branchen..."* — vague and unquantified; if the year is known, use it; if not, find something concrete
4. *"Vi tilbyder en bred vifte af services"* — "broad range of services" is padding
5. *"Kontakt os i dag for en uforpligtende snak"* — not wrong, but too formulaic; replace with something that names what the conversation is about

## 6. Page-by-Page Messaging Notes

### Homepage

**Primary message:** You are hiring a named craftsman with formal warranty protection — not an anonymous contractor.

**Conversion job:** Get the visitor to either call/email for a quote, or navigate deeper into a service or case that matches their project.

**Key proof points:**
- Byg Garanti badge + brief explanation (3yr/10yr)
- Dansk Byggeri logo
- Service tiles showing full scope
- Link to at least one case study

**Headline direction:** Lead with the identity + warranty combination. Not "Tømrer og snedker med erfaring" — instead, something that names the differentiation: the person, the guarantee, and the full scope. Example direction: *"[Name] — tømrer, snedker og Byg Garanti-certificeret."* Subheadline should name the geographic reach and call to action.

---

### Services Page (Hub + individual pages)

**Primary message:** One firm handles everything — from structural work to fine carpentry, and specialist remediation.

**Conversion job:** Match the visitor's project type to a specific service, then move them to contact.

**Key proof points:**
- The dual tømrer + snedker capability positioned explicitly
- Link each service to the relevant case study where one exists
- Byg Garanti coverage applies across all services — state this clearly

**Headline direction:** Each service page should open with the specific problem the homeowner has, not the service name. Not: "Tag-renovering." Instead: "Er dit tag ved at give slip?" (Is your roof starting to give way?) Then deliver the capability and credentials.

---

### Cases Page

**Primary message:** Real projects, real photographs, same warranty on every one.

**Conversion job:** Build confidence in scope and quality; surface the case that most closely matches the visitor's own project; prompt them to get in touch.

**Key proof points:**
- Authentic photography (no stock images)
- Breadth of project type (new build, extension, remediation — three different categories)
- Geographic breadth (Holstebro AND Copenhagen)
- Each case should close with a CTA: "Har du et lignende projekt? Kontakt os."

**Headline direction:** Each case should be framed as a narrative: the situation, the challenge, the result. Not just a project name. "Skimmelsvamp i Københavnerlejlighed — saneret og garanteret" is stronger than "Renovering, København."

---

### About Page

**Primary message:** This is Martin's business — a trained craftsman who puts his name on every job, backed by two decades of industry membership.

**Conversion job:** Build personal trust for visitors who need to know who they are hiring before they will call. The About page is the trust-conversion page.

**Key proof points:**
- Martin's name, training, and background (needs client input but frame must be ready)
- Byg Garanti and Dansk Byggeri explained — not just logos
- CVR number: "Vi er en registreret virksomhed — CVR 3646 6588"
- [NEEDS: Martin headshot] — a photo of Martin is the single highest-value trust asset for a named-person business

**Headline direction:** Avoid generic "Om os" (About us) heading as the primary message. Use Martin's name or a direct statement of accountability. Direction: "Manden bag håndværket" or simply lead with Martin's name and a one-sentence bio.

---

### Contact Page

**Primary message:** Reach the person who will do the work — not a receptionist, not a sales team.

**Conversion job:** Phone call or email enquiry. The page should remove all friction between interest and first contact.

**Key proof points:**
- Direct phone number and email (no contact form as the only option — Danish trade clients often prefer calling)
- Address and map (confirms physical, real business)
- CVR number (credibility anchor)
- Brief reassurance: "Vi svarer inden for [X] hverdage" — [NEEDS: response time commitment from client]

**Headline direction:** Not just "Kontakt os." Make it actionable and warm: "Fortæl os om dit projekt" or "Få et gratis tilbud." The headline should frame the first contact as the start of a conversation, not a form submission.

### Local SEO Signals to Include

These are on-page and structural elements that improve local search ranking:

1. **Name / Address / Phone (NAP) consistency** — Publish the full address (Tingagerparken 3, Mejdal/Halgård, 7500 Holstebro) and phone number consistently in the footer, contact page, and structured data. Match exactly what is on Google Business Profile.

2. **CVR number in structured data** — Include as part of Organization schema markup; signals a verified Danish legal entity.

3. **Byg Garanti and Dansk Byggeri as named entities** — These are recognised Danish certification bodies; naming them explicitly in copy (not just as logos) creates semantic association.

4. **Location mentions in body copy** — "Holstebro," "Midtjylland," and specific neighbouring towns (Herning, Struer, Ikast) should appear naturally in service and about copy, not just in meta tags.

5. **Case study location naming** — "Parcelhus i Holstebro," "Tilbygning i Halgård," "Lejlighed i København" — these create geo-relevant content that local search picks up.

6. **Google Business Profile** — [NEEDS: client to claim/update Google Business Profile] — if not already claimed, this is the single highest-ROI local SEO action. The profile should list all services, show the case study photos, and include the Byg Garanti certification note.

7. **Schema markup** — Implement `LocalBusiness` + `HomeAndConstructionBusiness` schema with `areaServed` set to both Holstebro/Midtjylland and "Denmark" (to reflect the nationwide reach). Include `hasCredential` for Byg Garanti.

8. **Page titles and meta descriptions** — Every service page title should include the service name + "tømrer" + the primary location. Example: `Tag-renovering | Tømrer Holstebro — Martin Mejdahl Jørgensen`.
